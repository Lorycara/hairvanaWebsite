Flattering Hairstyles for Diamond Face Shapes

Diamond face shapes are characterized by their striking angles and proportions. With narrow foreheads and jawlines paired with prominent cheekbones, diamond faces have a naturally dramatic and defined look. The right hairstyle can either soften these angular features or accentuate them, depending on your preference. This guide will help you discover the most flattering hairstyles to complement your unique diamond-shaped face.

How to Identify a Diamond Face Shape

You likely have a diamond face shape if:

  • Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face
  • You have a narrow forehead
  • Your jawline is narrow and potentially pointed
  • Your face length is longer than its width
  • Your chin is defined and may be slightly pointed

Top Hairstyles for Diamond Face Shapes

1. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs help balance a narrower forehead by adding width and softness. They draw attention to the eyes while creating the illusion of a wider forehead area, perfectly complementing the diamond face shape's natural angles.

2. Layered Bob

A layered bob that hits at the jawline or just below is ideal for diamond faces. The layers add volume at the sides of the face, balancing prominent cheekbones, while the length helps soften a pointed chin.

3. Chin-Length Cuts

Styles that hit right at the chin create balance with diamond face shapes by adding width where the face narrows. These cuts work particularly well with textured or tousled styling that adds volume around the jawline.

4. Textured Pixie

Pixie cuts with texture on top and longer pieces framing the face can beautifully complement diamond face shapes. The added height balances prominent cheekbones while softening angular features.

5. Soft Layers Around the Face

Medium to long hair with soft layers around the face helps soften the angles of a diamond face shape. These face-framing layers create movement and dimension while balancing prominent cheekbones.

6. Curtain Bangs

Curtain bangs that frame the face from a center part add width to the forehead while softening the appearance of high cheekbones. This style creates beautiful symmetry that complements diamond-shaped faces.

7. Long Waves or Curls

Long, voluminous hairstyles with waves or curls add softness to the angular features of diamond faces. The added volume at the sides creates balance with prominent cheekbones while the length elongates the face.

8. Short-to-Medium Shag

The layered, textured nature of a shag haircut is perfect for diamond faces. The choppy layers add volume at the forehead and jawline, creating balance with wider cheekbones and softening angular features.

9. Deep Side Part

A deep side part creates asymmetry that softens the structured appearance of a diamond face. This simple styling technique works with various lengths and adds volume to one side of the face, balancing prominent cheekbones.

10. Tousled Lob

A long bob (lob) with tousled texture adds width at the jawline while the casual, undone finish softens angular features. This versatile length works well for diamond faces as it adds volume where needed.

Styling Tips for Diamond Faces

  • Add volume at the forehead and jawline to balance prominent cheekbones
  • Soften angular features with textured styles and face-framing layers
  • Consider side parts that create asymmetry and soften sharp angles
  • Avoid styles that add too much height at the crown, which can elongate the face
  • Experiment with bangs to add width to a narrower forehead
  • Use soft waves or curls to add dimension and soften angular lines

What to Consider

While these recommendations highlight your best features, consider these factors when choosing a diamond face hairstyle:

  • Very sleek, flat styles can emphasize the angles of your face
  • Hairstyles with volume only at the crown can make your face appear more angular
  • Blunt-cut bangs may accentuate your cheekbones rather than balance them
  • Hair pulled straight back without volume on the sides can emphasize angular features

Remember, these are guidelines rather than rules. Your personal style preferences and hair texture should also influence your choice of hairstyle.
