Best Hairstyles for Round Face Shape

Find flattering hairstyles that add structure and length to your round face shape with these expert recommendations

Understanding Round Face Shape

A round face shape is characterized by soft curves and similar width and length measurements. With full cheeks and a rounded chin, round faces have gentle contours without sharp angles.

The right hairstyle can add the appearance of length and definition to your face, enhancing your natural features while creating more structure and dimension.

Characteristics of Round Face Shape

  • Face width and length are similar measurements
  • Full, curved cheeks that are the widest part of the face
  • Rounded jawline without sharp angles
  • Soft chin without pronounced points
  • Gentle curves throughout the face's contours

Recommended Hairstyles for Round Faces

Long Layers

Long, layered cuts that start below the chin help elongate the face while reducing width, creating a slimming effect.

Deep Side Part

A deep side part creates asymmetry that helps break up the roundness of the face, adding angular structure.

Angled Bob

An angled bob that's longer in front and shorter in back creates vertical lines that elongate a round face.

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs create diagonal lines across the face, adding angles that offset facial roundness.

Volume on Top

Styles with volume at the crown create height that elongates the face, balancing round proportions.

Textured Pixie

A pixie cut with longer, textured layers on top adds height and angles that complement a round face.

Textured Quiff

A quiff with height on top and shorter sides creates vertical lines that elongate a round face shape.

Side Part

A classic side part adds angles and asymmetry that help balance the soft curves of a round face.

Pompadour

The pompadour's height and vertical volume create the illusion of a longer face, offsetting roundness.

Angular Fringe

An angular fringe creates diagonals and sharp lines that contrast with and complement a round face shape.

Curly Top Fade

Keeping volume and height on top with faded sides adds length while minimizing width for round faces.

Textured Undercut

An undercut with textured length on top creates vertical dimension that elongates and balances a round face.

Styling Tips for Round Face Shape

  • Create height: Styles with volume on top elongate the face and balance roundness
  • Add angles: Choose cuts with angular elements to offset soft curves
  • Try side parts: Asymmetrical styles help break up circular proportions
  • Consider length: Hair that falls below the chin helps elongate the face
  • Avoid full circular shapes: Full, round styles can emphasize facial roundness
  • Frame strategically: Use layers that start at or below the cheekbones, not at the fullest part of the face
