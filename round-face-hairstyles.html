Best Hairstyles for Round Face Shape

Round face shapes are characterized by soft curves and equal proportions. With full cheeks and a rounded jawline, this face shape has a naturally youthful appearance. The key to finding flattering hairstyles for round faces is to create angles, length, and volume in the right places. This guide will help you discover the most complementary hairstyles that add definition and balance to your round face shape.

How to Identify a Round Face Shape

You likely have a round face shape if:

  • Your face width and length are similar in measurement
  • You have full cheeks that are the widest part of your face
  • Your jawline is softly curved rather than angular
  • Your forehead and jawline are approximately the same width
  • Your chin is rounded without sharp angles

Top Hairstyles for Round Face Shapes

1. Long Layers with Side Parting

Long, layered hairstyles that fall below the shoulders help elongate the face. A side part creates asymmetry that adds angles to a round face, while layers add dimension and movement that draw the eye vertically rather than horizontally.

2. Textured Lob (Long Bob)

A long bob that hits just below the chin helps create angles around a round face. Adding texture and choppy layers creates dimension while the length maintains a slimming effect on the face.

3. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs add diagonal lines to a round face, creating angles where there are naturally curves. This style helps draw attention to the eyes while visually elongating the face shape.

4. Pixie Cut with Height on Top

A pixie cut with volume and height on top creates vertical lines that lengthen a round face. Keeping the sides short and the top longer adds angles that complement the soft curves of a round face shape.

5. Asymmetrical Styles

Asymmetrical haircuts create angles and visual interest that balance out the symmetry of a round face. These styles can range from an asymmetrical bob to a dramatic uneven cut.

6. Face-Framing Layers

Layers that start at or below the chin frame the face and create vertical lines that slim and lengthen a round face shape. This technique works with various lengths and helps define the jawline.

7. Deep Side Part

A deep side part immediately creates asymmetry and angles that complement a round face. This simple styling technique works with many different hair lengths and adds height and volume where needed.

8. Voluminous Crown

Styles with added height and volume at the crown create vertical length that balances the width of a round face. Teasing the hair at the crown or using volumizing products can help achieve this effect.

9. Shaggy, Layered Medium Cuts

Medium-length shaggy cuts with lots of layers add texture and movement that draw attention away from the fullness of a round face. The varied lengths create vertical lines that visually slim the face.

10. Long, Straight Styles

Long, sleek hairstyles with minimal layers create vertical lines that elongate a round face shape. The length draws the eye downward, creating the illusion of a longer, slimmer face.

Styling Tips for Round Faces

  • Create height and volume at the crown to elongate your face
  • Opt for side parts rather than center parts to create asymmetry
  • Choose styles that fall below the chin to create length
  • Use layering techniques to add angles and definition
  • Incorporate texturizing products to add dimension and movement
  • Consider angled bangs or side-swept fringe rather than straight-across bangs

What to Consider

While these recommendations highlight your best features, consider these factors when choosing a round face hairstyle:

  • Very short haircuts without height on top can emphasize the roundness of your face
  • Chin-length bobs with no layers may accentuate the fullness of your cheeks
  • Straight-across bangs can make your face appear wider and shorter
  • Center parts can emphasize symmetry, which may accentuate roundness
  • Extremely curly styles with width at the cheeks may add horizontal volume

Remember, these are guidelines rather than rules. Your personal style preferences and hair texture should also influence your choice of hairstyle.
