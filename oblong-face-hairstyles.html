Best Hairstyles for Oblong Face Shape

Find the perfect hairstyles to balance and flatter your oblong face shape with these expert recommendations

Understanding Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face shape (also called rectangular) is characterized by its length being noticeably greater than its width. The forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are typically similar in width, creating a long, straight appearance down the sides of the face.

With the right hairstyle, you can create the illusion of width and soften the elongated appearance of your face, highlighting your best features.

Characteristics of Oblong Face Shape

  • Face length noticeably longer than face width
  • Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline similar in width
  • Long, straight cheeks
  • Less pronounced chin compared to other face shapes
  • High forehead

Recommended Hairstyles for Oblong Faces

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs create width at the forehead and break up the length of an oblong face, softening features and adding dimension.

Layered Mid-Length Cut

Medium-length cuts with layers around the cheeks and jaw add width to the face, balancing proportions and adding softness.

Wavy Bob

A wavy bob that hits at the chin or shoulders adds width and texture, creating the illusion of a shorter, more balanced face shape.

Voluminous Pixie

A pixie cut with volume on top and longer sides creates width at the temples and cheekbones, helping to balance a longer face shape.

Textured Shag

A shaggy cut with lots of layers throughout creates fullness at the sides of the face, minimizing length and adding youthful volume.

Curtain Bangs

Curtain bangs frame the face beautifully, shortening the appearance of an oblong face while drawing attention to the eyes and cheekbones.

Middle Part

A middle part with volume on the sides creates width that helps balance the length of an oblong face shape.

Textured Fringe

A textured fringe that sits across the forehead shortens the face visually and adds width at the top.

Curly Volume

Natural curls or waves add width to the sides of the face, helping to create balance for an oblong face shape.

Classic Crop

A cropped style with fuller sides helps add width while keeping a clean, classic look that flatters an oblong face.

Textured Layers

Textured, layered styles with volume on the sides create width that balances longer face proportions.

Modern Cut

A modern cut with volume on top and fullness at the sides breaks up vertical lines and adds beneficial width.

Styling Tips for Oblong Face Shape

  • Add volume at the sides: Styles with width at the cheeks help balance your face's length
  • Consider bangs: Straight-across or side-swept bangs can shorten the appearance of your face
  • Avoid excessive length: Very long, straight hair can elongate your face further
  • Try layers: Layers that start around the jawline add width and soften features
  • Experiment with waves: Wavy textures create the illusion of width and fullness
  • Middle part caution: A center part can emphasize length; consider a side part instead
