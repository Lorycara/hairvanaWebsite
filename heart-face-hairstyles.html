Best Hairstyles for Heart Face Shape

Heart face shapes are characterized by a wider forehead that gradually narrows to a pointed chin. This romantic face shape, sometimes called the inverted triangle, has naturally high cheekbones and often a widow's peak hairline. The key to finding flattering hairstyles for heart faces is to balance the width at the forehead while accentuating your best features. This guide will help you discover the most complementary hairstyles for your heart-shaped face.

How to Identify a Heart Face Shape

You likely have a heart face shape if:

  • Your forehead is the widest part of your face
  • Your face gradually narrows from the forehead to the chin
  • Your chin is pointed or narrower compared to your forehead
  • You have high, well-defined cheekbones
  • You may have a widow's peak (a V-shape) at your hairline

Top Hairstyles for Heart Face Shapes

1. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs help balance the width of a heart-shaped face by softening the forehead. These bangs create movement and draw attention to the eyes while minimizing a wider forehead.

2. Layered Mid-Length Cuts

Medium-length cuts with layers that start at the chin or below help add width to the lower part of the face. This balances the natural width at the forehead and complements a pointed chin.

3. Long, Wavy Styles

Long, flowing waves help soften the angles of a heart-shaped face. The added volume around the jawline creates balance with a wider forehead, while the movement of the waves adds softness to a pointed chin.

4. Chin-Length Bob

A bob that hits precisely at the chin adds width exactly where heart faces need it most. This creates balance with the wider forehead while accentuating the cheekbones and jawline.

5. Deep Side Part

A deep side part works well for heart faces as it directs attention away from the width of the forehead. This simple styling technique can be used with many different lengths and adds asymmetry that flatters heart shapes.

6. Textured Pixie Cut

Pixie cuts with longer, textured pieces on top and around the temples help balance a heart-shaped face. The shorter sides slim the width at the forehead while the added texture softens a pointed chin.

7. Wispy, Face-Framing Layers

Soft, face-framing layers that start at the cheekbones and continue downward help add width to the lower portion of a heart-shaped face. These layers create harmony and balance with the natural facial structure.

8. Curtain Bangs

Curtain bangs that frame the face from a center part add softness to the forehead area while highlighting the cheekbones. This style is particularly flattering for heart shapes with a widow's peak.

9. Tousled Lob (Long Bob)

A shoulder-length lob with tousled texture adds width at the jawline while creating movement that complements the angular features of a heart-shaped face. The casual, undone finish softens a pointed chin.

10. Full, Bouncy Curls

Voluminous curls that are fuller at the jawline than at the forehead create balance for heart-shaped faces. This style adds width where needed while the volume helps soften a pointed chin.

Styling Tips for Heart Faces

  • Add width and volume around the jawline and chin area
  • Consider bangs or fringe that soften the forehead width
  • Opt for styles with fullness at the sides near the jawline
  • Use side parts to create asymmetry and balance
  • Choose cuts that highlight your naturally high cheekbones
  • Experiment with soft layers that frame the face

What to Consider

While these recommendations highlight your best features, consider these factors when choosing a heart face hairstyle:

  • Very full, voluminous styles at the crown can emphasize forehead width
  • Straight-across blunt bangs may accentuate the width of your forehead
  • Tight, sleek styles pulled back from the face can emphasize a pointed chin
  • Very short cuts without softness around the face may not balance your proportions
  • Center parts without bangs can emphasize the symmetry and width of your forehead

Remember, these are guidelines rather than rules. Your personal style preferences and hair texture should also influence your choice of hairstyle.
