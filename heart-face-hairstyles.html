Best Hairstyles for Heart Face Shape

Discover flattering haircuts that balance your wider forehead and narrow chin with these expert recommendations

Understanding Heart Face Shape

A heart-shaped face is characterized by a wider forehead that gradually narrows to a pointed chin. With prominent cheekbones and a defined jawline that tapers, this face shape resembles an inverted triangle or heart.

The right hairstyle can help balance the proportions of your face, softening the wider upper area while adding volume around the narrower chin to create harmony.

Characteristics of Heart Face Shape

  • Wider forehead that forms the broadest part of the face
  • Cheekbones that are high and well-defined
  • Jawline that narrows significantly toward the chin
  • Pointed or narrow chin
  • Face width decreases from forehead to chin

Recommended Hairstyles for Heart Faces

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs help soften a wider forehead while drawing attention away from a narrower chin area.

Chin-Length Bob

A bob that hits at the chin creates the illusion of width in the lower face, balancing heart-shaped proportions.

Medium Layers

Face-framing layers that start at the cheekbones and continue downward add fullness around the chin.

Long Waves

Soft waves that add volume around the jaw and chin area help balance the wider upper portion of the face.

Textured Pixie

A pixie with longer, textured pieces at the top helps balance the forehead width while keeping sides softer.

Deep Side Part

A deep side part creates asymmetry that helps balance wider foreheads while adding softness to features.

Textured Fringe

A textured fringe helps soften a wider forehead while creating balance for heart-shaped proportions.

Medium Sweep

A medium length style swept to the side helps minimize forehead width while framing facial features.

Short Sides, Longer Top

Keeping the sides short with more length on top creates vertical balance for heart-shaped faces.

Classic Side Part

A side part with medium length on top helps redirect attention from a wider forehead.

Soft Taper

A tapered cut with softer edges complements the natural angles of a heart-shaped face.

Wavy Texture

Natural waves with volume on the sides help balance the narrower chin of a heart-shaped face.

Styling Tips for Heart Face Shape

  • Balance proportions: Add volume around the jawline and chin to balance the wider forehead
  • Soften the forehead: Side-swept or textured bangs help minimize a broader forehead
  • Create width at jaw: Styles that hit at or below the chin add visual width to the lower face
  • Avoid excessive volume: Too much volume at the crown can emphasize an already wider forehead
  • Consider layers: Face-framing layers help soften angular features and add dimension
  • Try side parts: Side parts help break up the symmetry of the face and balance proportions
