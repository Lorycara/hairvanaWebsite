Best Haircuts for Men with Round Faces

Finding the right haircut for a round face shape is all about creating structure and definition. Round faces are characterized by soft angles and equal proportions, with the width and length of the face being roughly the same. The ideal haircuts for men with round faces create the illusion of length and angles to add definition to softer features. This comprehensive guide will help you discover the most flattering styles for your round face shape.

How to Identify a Round Face Shape

You likely have a round face shape if:

  • The width and length of your face are approximately equal
  • Your face has a rounded jawline without sharp angles
  • Your cheeks are the widest part of your face or are full
  • You have a rounded chin without a pointed tip
  • Your hairline is typically rounded or circular

Top Haircuts for Men with Round Faces

1. Pompadour

The pompadour adds significant height at the top of the head, creating the illusion of a longer face. This classic style works well for round faces as it draws the eye upward and creates vertical lines that help elongate the face's appearance.

2. Undercut with Textured Top

An undercut features short sides with length on top, creating contrast that adds angles to a round face. The textured top adds volume and height, while the shorter sides slim the face's silhouette.

3. Side-Parted Quiff

The side-parted quiff combines height at the front with a clean side part, creating asymmetry that complements round faces. This style adds structure and makes the face appear longer rather than wider.

4. Angular Fringe

An angular fringe that comes across the forehead at a diagonal can add sharpness to softer features. This style creates visual interest and angles that balance the roundness of the face.

5. Faux Hawk

The faux hawk features shorter sides with longer hair styled upward in the center, creating a vertical line that elongates the face. This edgy style adds angles and structure to round faces.

6. Textured Crop

A textured crop with forward styling and a rougher texture creates dimension that complements round faces. The textured fringe can be styled to add angles to the forehead, while the sides are kept shorter.

7. Slicked Back Undercut

Slicking back the hair creates length and draws attention upward, making this an excellent choice for round faces. The contrast between the slicked-back top and short sides adds definition to softer features.

8. Comb Over with Fade

A comb over with a high or mid fade creates clean lines and asymmetry that balances round face shapes. The diagonal line of the comb over adds angles, while the fade slims the sides of the face.

Styling Tips for Round Faces

  • Add height at the top to create the illusion of a longer face
  • Keep the sides shorter than the top to slim the face's silhouette
  • Incorporate asymmetrical elements like side parts or diagonal fringes
  • Use texturizing products to create definition and angles
  • Consider keeping some facial hair to add structure to the jawline
  • Avoid styles that add width to the sides of the face

Beard Styles for Round Faces

The right beard style can significantly enhance a round face shape by adding definition and structure to the jawline:

  • Extended Goatee: Adds length to the chin while keeping the sides clean
  • Diagonal Beard: Creates angles along the cheeks rather than following the natural curve
  • Van Dyke: Adds length without width, perfect for round faces
  • Short Boxed Beard: Creates a more defined jawline when kept neat and angular
  • Stubble with Defined Lines: Adds subtle definition without overwhelming softer features

What to Consider

While these recommendations highlight your best features, consider these factors when choosing a round face haircut:

  • Very short, all-one-length haircuts can emphasize facial roundness
  • Bowl cuts or straight bangs that follow the curve of the face can accentuate roundness
  • Styles with too much volume on the sides can make the face appear wider
  • Very long hair that hangs straight down without layers can emphasize roundness

Remember, these are guidelines rather than rules. Your personal style preferences and hair texture should also influence your choice of haircut.
